**Big Beefy Bob** is a half-orc warrior and front-line combatant whose immense strength and resilience make him a cornerstone of the party. Present at the heist of the [[Temple to Tolaume]], he becomes closely tied to the elemental conflict after coming into possession of the [[Elements#Soul Stone|Soul Stone]], an event triggered by its protective mechanisms.

Throughout the campaign, Bob experiences increasingly intense supernatural phenomena, including recurring “Dead Dreams” and direct communication from entities such as the [[First Puli]]. These visions, combined with his role as both protector and investigator, such as identifying the killer aboard [[The Zoraneset]], position him as a figure deeply connected to death, memory, and the unseen forces shaping the world.

# Backstory
When Bob was young his parents gave him to [[Cult of Paroshinat|a cult]]. His parents had put him into the cult as he was very boisterous and strong, and they felt like he was "too much to handle." When he joined the cult, that was when he was given the name Big Beefy Bob. It was also when he was given the little orange hat that he always wears.

Big Beefy Bob cannot remember anything from his past, and believes he was born in the cult, and that he was only brought to life to do what the cult leader tells him to. He doesn't really understand what a family actually is.

Bob is mainly adventuring as he was out recruiting suitable people for his cult; looking for people he feels like would be a good fit. He is also enjoying the opportunity to learn about the world around him more and the world outside the cult, exploring magic and new environments.

# In the Campaign

## Session 1 — The Stones in Amphos
Big Beefy Bob enters the story in the [[Northern Slums]] marketplace of [[Amphos]], where he becomes an early, unwitting participant in the chaos to come — [[Riley - Player Notes|Riley]] leaps over him and steals [[Big Beefy Bob's Little Orange Hat|his little orange bowler hat]], which Bob promptly recovers after the temple heist later that night. That hat, adorned with a symbol of the cult he was once part of, will quietly unsettle at least one party member long before its significance is understood.

At midnight, Bob serves as the heist's muscle, taking out the temple guard to allow the party entry into the inner chamber. Once inside the vault, he follows [[Ellowen Aurelian Vale|Ellowen]] and picks up a black stone — breaking it. Whatever that stone was, it is gone. But something else is waiting for him. The [[Elements#Soul Stone|Soul Stone]] is locked behind the door of [[Academy of the Arcane in Amphos|the Academy]]'s Protected Archive, and when the party make their way there following the heist, Bob feels it before he sees it. He begins banging on the door, drawn to the stone on the other side by a pull he cannot explain. When Riley's disastrous attempt to break into the archive triggers the Soul Stone's protection mechanism, the stone launches itself to its chosen protector: Big Beefy Bob. It chooses him.

He also finds [[Frank the Rock]] outside the temple on the ground — a small rock of uncertain origin with worn lettering carved on its rear — and puts googly eyes on it. Frank becomes a companion of sorts.

The party flee Amphos north-west with arrest warrants on their heads. At [[The Heavy Head Inn]], while Ellowen sleeps, her Mage Hand buries Bob's orange hat deep in Riley's bag — an unconscious act that hints at something about that hat Ellowen recognises but cannot yet name.

## Session 2 — The Tavern

The next morning at the inn, a hooded figure is seated alone on the upper landing, writing. Bob, unaware that this quiet stranger is in fact the [[The Prince - Player Notes|Prince of Amphos]] travelling in disguise — and apparently hungry — begins feeding him gruel. The prince begrudgingly accepts it. From this entirely one-sided interaction, Bob declares him his "second bestest bestie," with Ellowen holding the position of "first bestest bestie." The prince's true identity remains entirely unknown to Bob throughout this encounter and to the present.

The party take on a rat-clearing job to obtain a letter of passage and then probe the inn's locked guest rooms, discovering a lute and finding a pirate-related letter establishing their next destination: [[Aklebe]].

## Session 3 — The Path

Travelling north toward Aklebe, the party discover a broken tombstone bearing the name of the legendary ancient traveller [[Hasharshagetes]], and a Bon stone circle whose mossy stones correspond to the elemental aspects. The land is old and charged in ways the party are only beginning to understand.

In the forest, the ambush comes fast. A pack of wolves tears into the group, and Big Beefy Bob absorbs the worst of it — he is left critically injured, taking repeated attacks across the fight while others deal damage around him. He survives, but the encounter underlines just how brutal life on the road has become.

After tending wounds, the party find their way to an enormous golden tree in a forest clearing, its roots drawing on magically charged stones deep underground. When Bob touches it, he does not get elemental communion or physical transformation. He gets visions — repeated images seen through the eyes of a long-dead figure: mountains, altars, sealed gates, and the containment of catastrophic elemental power. These are not glimpses of the present. They are history, or something older, pressing through the Soul Stone's connection to the dead and the forgotten. He sees things the others do not, and the weight of it does not leave him.

That night in Aklebe, most of the party sleep normally. Bob does not. Whispering voices offer power in his dreams while Ellowen's book speaks cryptically of memory, loss, and forgotten names. During the night, through some magical mishap, his little orange hat begins to glow with a bright light — unnoticed by the sleeping party around him.

## Session 4 — Aklebe, The Panting Shadow, and a Game of Cups

Ellowen wakes before the others and notices the hat still glowing with blinding intensity. The party make their way to [[The Panting Shadow]] gambling den to win back the [[The Zoraneset|Harbormaster's ship]]. At the bar, the Prince is quietly present again, participating in the game of cups. Despite Bob's approaches, the prince remains characteristically quiet. He wins the game alongside Ellowen.

In the high-stakes Poison Roulette game — 28 drinks, six laced with mugwort — Bob takes the brunt alongside [[Evangeline Marquis|Yvaquis]], eventually collapsing into a groggy, poisoned stupor. Despite this, the party secure the Imperial War Galley _The Zoraneset_ and depart Aklebe with a new ally, the Firbolg [[Masha|Masha]], aboard.

## Session 5 — The Zoraneset

Aboard the _Zoraneset_, with only 150 of a required 200 crew, the party take on officer roles. Bob is appointed First Mate, tasked with keeping crew morale high. He refuses to remove his glowing orange hat despite Captain [[Mari Shkari]]'s explicit and repeated complaints. The tension between Bob and the captain over the hat establishes one of the session's background conflicts.

When a Giant Octopus attacks the ship — smashing oars, killing rowers, and breaching the hull — Bob enters a rage and hammers the creature with his axes. In a characteristically Bob finale, he attempts to give the terrified octopus a hug while still holding his axes, inadvertently executing it in the embrace.

That night, the party eat the octopus. The captain tells an ancient legend about seven elementals who once nearly toppled an empire, and warns the party that the old Republic used to execute elementals. Bob goes to sleep. And in his sleep, for the first time with clarity, a singular voice speaks to him directly in the Dead Dreams. It introduces itself as the First Puli and promises to help him, if he listens. It is not the murmuring chorus of the dreams before. This voice is clear, purposeful, and singular. It knows his name.

## Session 6 — Murder on The Zoraneset

At 5:00 AM, a scream wakes the ship. Captain Mari Shkari is found dead on the lower deck, his body dragged and washed down with stolen spirits. The ship descends into suspicion.

Bob is not without motive in the eyes of the investigation — the captain had insulted his little orange hat. But the evidence, painstakingly assembled by the party, points elsewhere. Bob is on the 02:00 watch with Ellowen and is not present when the murder happens. The investigation converges on [[Dashos]]: a crew member who missed his 4:00 AM watch, smells of the herbs belonging to Yvaquis, has a freshly cleaned hat and clothes, and was seen dragging the captain from his bed around 04:30.

During the investigation, Ellowen uses Disguise Self to take on the appearance of the dead Mari Shkari. When Bob discovers her in this form, Ellowen, who has to try and fool Bob, offers the memorable assessment that Mari Shkari has "a lot of blood" and that it "runs in the family." His Dead Dreams continue throughout, becoming noticeable enough to his companions that the supernatural voices are no longer something he can easily conceal.

At [[Pithertara]], Bob reaches the conclusion that Dashos is the killer and hands him over to the local authorities. As the suspect is processed, [[The Tagaminase]] — imperial soldiers — arrive and arrest Masha for her involvement in the bombing of [[The Great Palace of Amphos]]. She fights for her escape and is beheaded. It is the first death in the party's close circle, and Bob was there when it happened.
